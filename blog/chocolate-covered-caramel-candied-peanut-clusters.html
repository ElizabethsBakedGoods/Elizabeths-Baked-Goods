Chocolate Covered Caramel Candied Peanut Clusters – A Sweet Holiday Accident!

Published December 2025 | By Elizabeth

How These Clusters Came to Be

It all started one chilly December afternoon. I was attempting to make classic peanut brittle for a holiday gift, carefully following my old recipe. Or at least I thought I was…

In my holiday-induced distraction (and maybe a little too much coffee), I completely forgot to add the baking soda. Instead of the airy, crackling brittle I was expecting, I ended up with sticky, golden caramel-coated peanuts. My first thought? Disaster. But then I dipped a few in chocolate—and magic happened.

What I thought was a mistake became a holiday favorite: chocolate covered caramel candied peanut clusters. Sweet, crunchy, chocolatey, and addictive—they quickly became my go-to gift and festive snack.

Why They’re Perfect for the Holidays

  • Easy to make: No complicated candy thermometer needed—just caramel, peanuts, and chocolate.
  • Gifts everyone loves: Pack them in cute jars or festive bags for coworkers, neighbors, or friends.
  • Festive and fun: They’re the perfect sweet treat to enjoy while wrapping gifts or cozying up with holiday movies.
  • Sweet & salty magic: The caramel and chocolate perfectly complement the crunchy peanuts, creating that irresistible flavor combination.

The Recipe

Ready to make your own accidental holiday magic? Here’s how:

  • 1 cup roasted peanuts (unsalted or lightly salted)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • Optional: sea salt for sprinkling
  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium saucepan, melt sugar, butter, and salt over medium heat until golden brown.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Add peanuts and stir to coat evenly in the caramel.
  5. Drop spoonfuls onto the baking sheet and let cool 20–30 minutes.
  6. Melt chocolate chips in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  7. Dip clusters into the melted chocolate or drizzle over them.
  8. Optional: sprinkle with a pinch of sea salt.
  9. Let chocolate set completely before serving or gifting.

Holiday Tips & Ideas

  • Package clusters in festive jars or clear cellophane bags for last-minute gifts.
  • Try mixing in a few holiday sprinkles or crushed candy canes for extra festive flair.
  • Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week—if they last that long!
