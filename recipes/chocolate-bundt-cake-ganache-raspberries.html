Chocolate Bundt Cake with Chocolate Ganache & Raspberries

*Photo shows a mini version of this recipe

A Chocolate Bundt Cake That Looks as Good as It Tastes

There's something truly special about a chocolate Bundt cake. With its sculpted shape, rich cocoa flavor, and tender crumb, it's a cake that looks impressive without requiring complicated decorating techniques. This is exactly what I was looking for when I set out to create the perfect chocolate Bundt cake recipe.

What makes this recipe so reliable is the balance of ingredients. I use cocoa powder instead of melted chocolate, which gives us a lighter texture while still delivering deep chocolate flavor. The combination of hot coffee and buttermilk keeps the cake moist without making it dense. When you pair this with a silky chocolate ganache, a dusting of powdered sugar, and fresh raspberries, you get a dessert that feels both elegant and approachable.

I've baked this cake dozens of times for family gatherings, celebrations, and just because I craved chocolate. Every single time, it delivers consistent, beautiful results. The Bundt pan shape ensures even baking and that beautiful presentation straight from the oven‚Äîno complicated frosting techniques needed.

Whether you're a home baker looking for a dependable chocolate cake recipe, a small business baker needing something that performs consistently, or simply someone who loves chocolate, this recipe is for you. Below, you'll find detailed instructions, helpful baking tips, ingredient explanations, and answers to frequently asked questions to help ensure your success every time.

  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in vanilla extract.

  10. To make ganache: Heat heavy cream until steaming. Pour over chopped chocolate in a bowl. Let sit 1 minute, then stir until smooth. Cool for 5 minutes.

  12. Dust with powdered sugar and top with fresh raspberries. Serve and enjoy!

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes this chocolate Bundt cake moist?

The combination of oil, buttermilk, and hot coffee keeps the cake moist without making it dense. The hot liquid also helps bloom the cocoa flavor.

Can I make this cake ahead of time?

Yes! Bake the cake 1‚Äì2 days ahead and store covered at room temperature or in the refrigerator. Add ganache and toppings shortly before serving for best presentation.

Do I have to use coffee in this recipe?

No. Coffee enhances the chocolate flavor but does not make the cake taste like coffee. You may substitute hot water if preferred.

Why is my Bundt cake sticking to the pan?

This usually happens due to insufficient pan greasing. Make sure every groove of the Bundt pan is thoroughly coated. Dusting with cocoa powder also helps.

Can I freeze chocolate Bundt cake?

Yes. The unfrosted cake freezes well for up to 2 months when wrapped tightly. Thaw completely before adding ganache and toppings.

Can I customize this cake for special occasions?

Absolutely! This recipe can be adapted with different toppings, fillings, or decorations to suit birthdays, holidays, and events.

Is chocolate ganache the same as chocolate frosting?

No. Ganache is made with chocolate and cream, giving it a smoother, richer texture than traditional butter-based frosting.

What type of cocoa powder works best?

Both natural and Dutch-processed cocoa can be used. Natural cocoa provides a more pronounced chocolate flavor, while Dutch-processed offers a smoother taste.

Storage & Shelf Life

Store baked cake at room temperature covered with plastic wrap or in a cake container for up to 2 days. Refrigerate for up to 4 days. For longer storage, wrap the unfrosted cake tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 2 months. Thaw completely at room temperature before adding ganache and toppings. The unfrosted cake stays fresh thanks to the moisture from the coffee and buttermilk.
