My Top 5 Mistakes Beginners Make When Baking Macarons

Published December 2025 | By Elizabeth

Macarons are one of the most beautiful and intimidating desserts out there. But once you learn the technique, they're completely doable at home. If you're struggling to get perfect feet, smooth shells, or the right texture, chances are you're making one of these common mistakes.

1. Skipping the Sift

Properly sifted almond flour and powdered sugar make all the difference. Any clumps can cause cracked shells or bumpy tops. Even if the flour says "fine," always sift—sometimes twice.

2. Overmixing or Undermixing

Macaronage—the mixing stage—is everything. The batter should flow slowly like lava. Overmixing makes the batter runny and flat. Undermixing makes thick batter that won't settle smoothly.

3. Not Resting the Shells

After piping, macarons need time to form a skin. This helps create the iconic feet. Depending on humidity, rest them anywhere from 20 minutes to an hour. If the tops are still tacky, they aren't ready yet.

4. Incorrect Oven Temperature

Ovens lie. Even if you set it to 300°F, it might run 20 degrees hotter or colder. Using an oven thermometer helps ensure consistent results. Too hot = cracked shells. Too cool = no feet.

5. Baking on Humid Days

Humidity is the enemy of macarons. Moisture in the air slows down the drying process. If you live in a humid area, turn on a fan or dehumidifier during the resting stage.

Success Tip

Macarons get easier every time you make them. Keep notes, adjust your technique, and don't get discouraged—every baker goes through this learning curve.
