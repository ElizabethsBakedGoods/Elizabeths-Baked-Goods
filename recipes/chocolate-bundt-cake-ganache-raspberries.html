Chocolate Bundt Cake with Chocolate Ganache, Powdered Sugar & Fresh Raspberries

By Elizabeth's Baked Goods | Updated January 11, 2026

Introduction

There are certain desserts that feel timeless—reliable, elegant, and comforting all at once. A chocolate Bundt cake is one of those desserts. With its sculpted shape, rich cocoa flavor, and tender crumb, it's a cake that looks impressive without requiring complicated decorating techniques. When paired with a silky chocolate ganache, lightly dusted powdered sugar, and fresh raspberries, this cake becomes both indulgent and refined.

This recipe page is designed for home bakers, small business bakers, and dessert lovers who want a dependable chocolate cake recipe that delivers consistent results. Whether you're baking for a family gathering, a celebration, or simply because you crave chocolate, this chocolate Bundt cake strikes the perfect balance between rich flavor and approachability.

Below, you'll find a detailed chocolate Bundt cake recipe, a smooth chocolate ganache recipe, and serving suggestions using powdered sugar and raspberries. This page also includes helpful baking tips, ingredient explanations, storage guidance, and frequently asked questions to help ensure your success every time you bake.

Why This Chocolate Bundt Cake Works

This chocolate Bundt cake recipe is carefully balanced to create a moist, tender crumb without being dense or dry. Using cocoa powder instead of melted chocolate allows the chocolate flavor to shine while keeping the texture light. The Bundt pan shape ensures even baking and a beautiful presentation straight from the oven.

The chocolate ganache adds a luxurious finish that enhances the cake without overpowering it. Powdered sugar provides a classic bakery-style look, while raspberries bring brightness and contrast to the rich chocolate flavor.

This combination makes the cake ideal for:

  • Dinner parties and holidays
  • Birthdays and special occasions
  • Bake sales and markets
  • Elegant desserts without complex decoration

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Wet Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup light brown sugar, packed
  • ¾ cup vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 cup buttermilk, room temperature
  • 1 cup hot coffee or hot water
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

For the Pan

  • Nonstick cooking spray or softened butter
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting the pan)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Thoroughly grease a 10–12 cup Bundt pan with nonstick spray or butter, making sure to coat all grooves. Dust lightly with cocoa powder and tap out any excess.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a separate large mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vegetable oil until smooth and well combined.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. The mixture should become slightly thick and glossy.
  5. Stir in the vanilla extract and buttermilk until fully incorporated.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing just until combined. Avoid overmixing to keep the cake tender.
  7. Slowly pour in the hot coffee or hot water, whisking gently. The batter will be thin—this is expected and helps create a moist cake.
  8. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared Bundt pan.
  9. Bake for 45–55 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs but no wet batter.
  10. Remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for 15 minutes.
  11. Carefully invert the cake onto a wire rack and allow it to cool completely before adding ganache or toppings.

Chocolate Ganache Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, finely chopped or chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Place the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl and set aside.
  2. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Do not let it boil.
  3. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate. Cover the bowl and let it sit undisturbed for 3–5 minutes.
  4. Gently whisk the mixture until the chocolate is completely melted and the ganache is smooth and glossy.
  5. Allow the ganache to cool slightly before pouring so it thickens to your desired consistency.

Finishing the Cake

Toppings

  • Chocolate ganache
  • Powdered sugar
  • Fresh raspberries

Once the cake is completely cool, spoon or pour the chocolate ganache over the top, allowing it to naturally drip down the sides. Let the ganache set for about 10–15 minutes.

Lightly dust the cake with powdered sugar just before serving. Garnish with fresh raspberries, placing them around the base of the cake or on top for a polished, bakery-style finish.

Ingredient Notes and Baking Tips

Cocoa Powder

Use unsweetened cocoa powder for best results. Dutch-processed cocoa will produce a slightly smoother flavor, while natural cocoa powder offers a more intense chocolate taste.

Coffee in Chocolate Cake

Hot coffee enhances the chocolate flavor without making the cake taste like coffee. If you prefer not to use coffee, hot water works well as a substitute.

Buttermilk

Buttermilk adds moisture and tenderness to the cake. If you don't have buttermilk, you can substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of vinegar or lemon juice to 1 cup of milk and letting it sit for 5 minutes.

Bundt Pan Preparation

Properly greasing and dusting your Bundt pan is essential. Cocoa powder prevents white residue and helps the cake release cleanly.

Storage and Make-Ahead Tips

Room Temperature

Store the cake covered at room temperature for up to 2 days.

Refrigerator

Store covered for up to 5 days. Allow the cake to come to room temperature before serving.

Freezing

The unfrosted cake can be wrapped tightly and frozen for up to 2 months. Thaw completely before adding ganache and toppings.

Ganache

Ganache can be made ahead and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Gently reheat before using.

Serving Suggestions

This chocolate Bundt cake pairs beautifully with:

  • Whipped cream
  • Vanilla ice cream
  • Espresso or coffee
  • Fresh berries

It works equally well as a casual dessert or a formal centerpiece.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What makes this chocolate Bundt cake moist?

A: This cake stays moist due to the combination of oil, buttermilk, and hot coffee or water. These ingredients help retain moisture and enhance the chocolate flavor without making the cake dense.

Q: Can I make this chocolate Bundt cake ahead of time?

A: Yes. This cake can be baked 1–2 days in advance and stored covered at room temperature or in the refrigerator. Add the ganache and toppings shortly before serving for best presentation.

Q: Do I have to use coffee in this recipe?

A: No. Coffee enhances the chocolate flavor but does not make the cake taste like coffee. You may substitute hot water if preferred.

Q: What type of cocoa powder works best for chocolate Bundt cake?

A: Unsweetened cocoa powder works best. Both natural and Dutch-processed cocoa can be used, depending on your flavor preference.

Q: How do I prevent my Bundt cake from sticking to the pan?

A: Thoroughly grease every crevice of the Bundt pan and dust with cocoa powder. Allow the cake to cool for 10–15 minutes before inverting.

Q: Can I freeze chocolate Bundt cake?

A: Yes. The unfrosted cake freezes well for up to 2 months when wrapped tightly. Thaw completely before adding ganache and toppings.

Q: Is chocolate ganache the same as chocolate frosting?

A: No. Ganache is made with chocolate and cream, giving it a smoother, richer texture than traditional butter-based frosting.

Q: Can I customize this cake for special occasions?

A: Absolutely. This recipe can be adapted with different toppings, fillings, or decorations to suit birthdays, holidays, and events.

Q: Can I make this cake without a Bundt pan?

A: Yes. You can bake this recipe in two 9-inch round pans or a 9×13-inch pan. Adjust baking time accordingly.

Q: Why is my Bundt cake sticking?

A: This usually happens due to insufficient pan greasing. Make sure every groove is coated thoroughly.

Q: Can I use milk chocolate for ganache?

A: You can, but the ganache will be sweeter and softer. Semi-sweet chocolate provides better balance.

Q: Can I add mix-ins?

A: Yes. Chocolate chips or chopped nuts can be folded into the batter before baking.
